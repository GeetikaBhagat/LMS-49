Intersting facts about Camel

  1. Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  2. There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels.
  3. Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat.
  4. The camel's hump is like a storage container. When camels use their stored fat, their hump will diminish. When they eat and drink again the hump will refill with fat.

Intersting facts about Chameleons

  1. There are more than 200 species of chameleons.
  2. 9 species of chameleons are listed as critically endangered.
  3. Chameleons are introduced to Florida, California, and Hawaii, California, and later kept as household pets.
  4. The chameleon’s eyes can transition between monocular vision (both eyes are used separately) and binocular vision which enables them to perceive a single three-dimensional image of its surroundings.
